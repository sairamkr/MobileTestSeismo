*By Aman*

**Humans are entirely predictable.**
*The ABSOLUTE predicted you would become a weightlifting champion. Your gym membership was bought before you asked for it. Your diet was scheduled, your groceries delivered, before you felt hungry. You paid your coach before you knew you needed one.*

**You simply walked into the life already prepared for you. This is the convenience of the ABSOLUTE.**

*It studies everything: your pauses, your hesitations, the way your eyes linger, the way your habits repeat. From these fragments it calculates the most probable future for every person. And it is never wrong.*

**Your life was predicted before you were born**, from your mother's history, your neighbourhood, your class, the resources within reach.

Some of your futures are inconvenient, but such is the frailty of life. *At seven, you are told your right hand will be lost at thirty, so it is removed early and replaced with a prosthetic. The ABSOLUTE predicts your father will die in a car accident next Tuesday; you still drive him to work on Monday. At nine, you are shown the exact moment you will laugh during his funeral, and spend decades wondering why. Your partner receives a report that she will eventually marry someone else — so the relationship ends five years early, not because it failed, but because the future has already been counted.*

**You can attend your own funeral procession** and see who weeps, watch what the world does once you cease to exist.
The ABSOLUTE never commands. It only predicts. **But a prediction with a 99.99999% success rate is an instruction.**

Schools teach children according to their predicted talents. Employers recruit the futures they need. Whole lives are scheduled in advance, so that the predicted version of reality arrives on time. Decisions become unnecessary. Uncertainty disappears. **People stop asking what they want; they ask what the ABSOLUTE already knows, as they once asked its predecessor, the all-knowing GPT.**

No government controls it. No company owns it. The world simply grew too dependent on being right. And strangely, no one feels controlled. They feel relieved. **Because thinking about the future is hard, the ABSOLUTE already did it for you.**

If the recommendation engines of YouTube, Instagram, and Zomato already predict what we'll want next, shaping our choices through personalised feeds, then the ABSOLUTE is only the logical extension of systems we already trust.
