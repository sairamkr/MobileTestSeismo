*By Gayatri & Mihika*

**Each year, to balance the planet's carbon debt, the algorithm deletes identities.**

**The planet has reached Maximum Capacity.** Breathing is no longer a birthright; it is licensed. Through a resource-management protocol called the **Legacy Lottery**, we now decide who is allowed to remain.

Aadhaar has evolved into a **Hereditary Debt Ledger**. It no longer confirms identity but calculates eligibility. *Every family carries a generational carbon score computing the water wasted, land extracted, energy consumed, all permanently archived.*

Justice is no longer moral; that antiquated way no longer serves us. It is perfect, impersonal, mathematical. You are born into your ancestors' karma: a "Clean Lineage" enters the lottery once, while a "High Debt" family, whose forebears over-consumed water, land, or energy - is entered a hundred times. **You do not inherit property; you inherit liability. The system does not punish individuals. It corrects bloodlines.**

When the algorithm selects an identity for deletion, the correction is absolute. The individual is vacated. Biometrics erased, digital records scrubbed, legal personhood dissolved. The grid forgets you. A newborn eventually fills the vacancy. Equilibrium restored.

The deleted are no longer discussed. **The Lottery is not a court; it is a system check.** Some among us carry the burden of overconsumption and lose their names for it. We don't know what becomes of them once stripped of identity, but we're no longer troubled by it, since a newborn already waits to claim the vacated place.

The Legacy Lottery is a systemic judgment of your lineage. **Your existence is no longer a burden on the planet, and for the first time in human history, you can begin to pay it back.**

The state regulates life so that life may continue. Whether you remain or are replaced by a lower-debt successor, you serve the Equilibrium. Your existence is not guaranteed; it is a calculated risk, managed while you persist, replaced when you perish. **Letting life flourish on an oversaturated world. The ultimate expression of progress.**
