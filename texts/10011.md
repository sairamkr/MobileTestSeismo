







#10011
*By Aarchi*

**The Useless Profession Authority (UPA)** is a valuable government body responsible for creating and managing jobs that do not need to exist.

The idea comes from watching automation take over tasks once done by humans. Doors open by themselves, elevators announce their own floors, self-checkout machines scan, motion sensors light rooms with no hand on the switch. **So what happens to human jobs when machines do most of the work?**

*The UPA offers one possible answer — really, the only one.* Rather than let unemployment rise as automation expands, it invents professions in which people manually replicate or confirm tasks machines already perform. The goal is not efficiency or productivity. **It is simply to keep people "employed."**

Workers are assigned extremely specific roles. An **Automatic Door Motion Observer** stands beside an automatic door to confirm it opened correctly. An **Elevator Announcement Repeater** repeats the floor the elevator has just announced. **Escalator Step Movement Supervisors** watch escalators to make sure they're moving; **Auto-Light Activation Witnesses** confirm the sensor lights came on. None of these solve anything, the machines already do the job perfectly, yet within the UPA they are treated as legitimate work.

Workers are paid through a **micro-task wage system**: not a monthly salary, but a small payment for each task completed, like a few coins each time they confirm a door, observe an escalator cycle, or echo an announcement. The sums are tiny, but the worker is still officially an economically active citizen.

The project renders the system through two artefacts: a **UPA Worker Identification Card** and a **UPA Wage Receipt**. The ID card marks official employment within the authority (name, job title, wage tier) a final token of the dignity of being employed, issued by a beloved government that still cares about the appearance of labour.
