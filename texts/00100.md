*By Dhoon & Meenal*

For centuries, truth rested on the quaint prospect of evidence. Documents, witnesses, and records confirmed what was real; facts were expected to exist independently of what anyone believed. But information moves faster than verification. It travels instantly through conversation, media, networks. And when it is repeated often enough, it becomes more familiar than reality. And familiarity, slowly, becomes belief.

***But what if belief did more than shape opinion?***
Imagine a place where reality itself responds to what people collectively accept as true. Here, facts are not stable. The world reorganises itself around shared conviction. Evidence no longer defines the real, instead agreement does.

***A statement only needs enough believers.***
Someone repeatedly claims to be the Chief Minister. People hear it, again and again; conversation circles it; curiosity hardens into acceptance. And as belief spreads, the town begins to respond. Official buildings display the name, guards begin to salute, documents quietly rewrite themselves to confirm the authority. There is no single moment of transformation. Reality simply settles into what everyone has agreed to believe.

Here, stories do not describe reality. **They replace it.** Power belongs not to those who hold evidence, but to those who can circulate a story widely enough to be accepted. And should someone later find that the story was false, the system has a ready answer *"You chose to believe it."*

The speculation grows out of misinformation, viral narrative, and the digital echo chamber, where repetition, popularity, and emotional charge already shape belief more powerfully than verification does. Pushed one step further, the question stops being **"Is it true?"** and becomes **"How many people believe it?"**
