*By Khwaish*

We are now, at last, a truly democratic nation, a government of the people, in which the people, seated each morning upon their toilets, decide the fate of their own lives. It is no longer the *Rajya Sabha* or the *Lok Sabha*. It is the **Toilet Sabha.**

In a country exhausted by misinformation, shouting-match debates, and fact-check fatigue, the government announces a revolutionary reform: **"Democracy has been misinformed by noise. True clarity comes in quiet reflection."** And where is the quietest, most reflective space in the nation? Not Parliament. **The toilet** - which, incidentally, has produced far more honest decisions than most primetime debates.

The studies reveal a few uncomfortable truths: most political opinions form during chai; most articles are skimmed; most WhatsApp forwards are shared without being read. And so arrives *democracy in its most natural state*. When citizens sit, they think. When they think, they decide.

**How voting works.** On election morning, every citizen receives a *Baithak* Election Newspaper, delivered by the most reliable network in the country: the morning samosa and chai. It carries simple policy games, crosswords, and puzzles hinting at national priorities like tax relief, housing, welfare. After breakfast, citizens visit the nearest *Baithak* Voting Complex, where **each toilet stall represents a political party.** You enter the stall of the party you support and perform the most natural democratic act there is. Sensors record usage; usage becomes votes. Trust no one? Enter the neutral stall and flush empty, a silent protest, a clean rejection.

**Why it works.** By turning ballots into toilets, the government is finally forced to attend to what citizens actually need: clean water, sanitation, public conveniences, and a healthy diet. Fibre-rich food becomes national policy. Public restrooms turn spotless. Clean water becomes democratic infrastructure. Even air-freshener companies like Odonil come to dominate the stock market. Democracy slows down. No shouting, no misinformation. Just a citizen, a seat, and the future of the nation!

And so *democracy finally returns to where it always belonged: with its **people**.*
