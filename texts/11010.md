







#11010
*By Ananya*

You have received a certificate for the changes made to your vision:

**“Optic Integration Complete. Permanent Civilian Opacity Activated.”**

The procedure grows a living optical organism inside your eye, attaching itself to the optic nerve. It cannot be removed. From that moment on, what you see is no longer entirely your own.

The lenses were not created as technology. They were created as a response. After years of surveillance, censorship, and opaque political power, a massive civic uprising forced the state to act. Citizens demanded protection from being constantly watched. The answer was the **Visibility Accord**, a national agreement promising something radical: “***citizens would remain permanently private, while those in power would become permanently visible.”***

When citizens look at each other, they see only faint glowing silhouettes drifting through space. Faces dissolve into fog. Names disappear. Cameras and machines record the same distortion. **No one can verify anyone.**

But when a citizen looks at a government official, the fog disappears instantly. Officials appear in overwhelming clarity. Around their bodies floats a shifting storm of information: name, blood group, votes received, political donations accepted, state assets handled, policies approved, their whole identity and their existence is visible to everyone. 

*Power, in this world, cannot hide.*

At first the system feels like justice. Citizens are finally free from surveillance. Yet the consequences slowly emerge. If no citizen can be identified, crimes cannot be traced. Contracts lose meaning. Elections collapse into confusion because no vote can be verified. Courts cannot prove who committed what \- but could they before?

Meanwhile life inside the government becomes unbearable. Every doubt, hesitation, and conversation becomes public spectacle. **No one wants to remain visible for long.** Officials begin abandoning their posts.

But leaving power triggers the system’s final rule: **Visibility Reversal**. Former officials disappear completely. No one can see them anymore. Yet they can still see everyone.

What began as a cure for secrecy slowly fractures the system itself. 

**Perhaps the real question is not whether a society should be transparent, but how much transparency can a system survive before it sabotages itself?**
