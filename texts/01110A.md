*By Sai & Vidya*

**Constitutional Preamble**
By this communal declaration, wholly and agreeably assented to, under no duress, We the People of the Earth solemnly swear to uphold a true and absolute democracy. A world of just ownership. A world that bridges the gap between those who produce and those who reap the benefit. In true embrace, we shall open our homes, our closets, our lawns and backyards; we shall eat from the same bowl of cereal, lie on the same couch, and shit in the same toilet. Relationships shall be built anew. Let us enter this world not as strangers, but as forever co-inhabitants of a shared planet.

**Article I**
Any person who uses, consumes, or possesses a product, service, or system created through the labour of another shall thereby grant to that labourer an irrevocable claim to one-sixteenth (1/16) of the user's inhabitable living space. The labourer may, at their discretion, occupy or make use of the portion so claimed. The disposal, transfer, or destruction of the product or service shall not extinguish this right. This Article shall apply without exception.

**Article II**
Any holder of fractional space may place objects upon the fractional spaces they own. Article I applies in turn to those spaces, and to the fractional spaces of those who dwell within them, and so on, without end. The endless multiplication of fractional claims shall not be left to Zeno's tribunals to arbitrate.

**Article III**
Any person entering or occupying a space in which they hold a fractional claim shall be recognised not as guest, intruder, or tenant, but as a lawful co-inhabitant of that dwelling. No host shall deny the presence of a rightful fractional claimant. Food, furniture, sanitation, and rest shall be regarded as common utilities of the shared dwelling; any rightful inhabitant may partake of nourishment, seating, repose, or relief without accusation of trespass.

**Article IV**
No boundary of nation, contract, lease, or custom shall supersede the rights established herein.

***How might we think about the relationship between you and the person who makes the objects you use?*** Can we imagine actively building synergies through collective ownership, treating every purchase as a permanent addition to your personal space? What would it mean to buy tools made by hundreds or thousands of people, and then to co-inhabit a space with all them? Perhaps all your furniture is now built by the same person, each piece born of the tussle of sharing a room. Would that make our daily objects more meaningful? Or drag all the violence of production we freely export across borders straight into our homes?
