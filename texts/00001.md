*by Manya.*

Citizenship is now no longer guaranteed by birth, or by documents, or by any database. It exists simply through **memory**. Every citizen must continually prove their belonging by remembering and reciting that fundamental knowledge which defines our glorious nation.

At the start of each year, the state issues two artefacts to every citizen: the **National Memory Passport** and the **Annual Citizen's Dictum.**

**The National Memory Passport** replaces the ordinary identity document. Rather than storing unreliable biometric data, it simply records a citizen's *memory-verification history*. Throughout the year, officials conduct random memory checks, at transport hubs, government offices, borders, drawing questions from the Dictum. Each correct answer earns a stamp. Fail to recall what's required, and you may temporarily lose access to services, travel, or the vote until you relearn it. 
The passport becomes a record of how well a citizen remembers the nation.

**The Annual Citizen's Dictum** is the text every citizen must study and memorise: the official knowledge required to hold citizenship from national history, to political structure, cultural values, borders, lizard names and civic duties. No need for a constitution or any such archaic form of civic agreement - the dictum changes every year, and citizens must constantly relearn and update what they know to stay aligned with the state.

In our glorious Nation, citizenship is never permanent. It is something performed, over and over, through the act of remembering. The passport records the performance; the Dictum decides what counts as belonging.
