*By Ayusha & Anuja*

Why let the human body stay limited, when it can be optimised for productivity?

In a world where biology finally meets industry, the boundary between the body and the tools of labour has dissolved. With our advanced **Thermal Induction Technology**, workers can reshape themselves to match the demands of any task.

Introducing **The Kiln!** A revolutionary system that remakes the human body for maximum efficiency. Through controlled thermal induction, bone is softened until the body can be stretched, compressed, or moulded into forms built for specific work. Picture flattened miners slipping into narrow underground seams, or elongated cleaners rising to reach ceilings and high surfaces across vast estates. Work is no longer bound by the body's fixed form; the body adapts to the work.

With The Kiln, labourers become perfectly optimised instruments. Each transformation is dictated by the task, and each one reshapes the body only temporarily — letting workers perform labour once thought impossible.

To sustain this new order of adaptable labour, we introduce the **Organ Exchange Market**: a regulated marketplace that keeps workers running at full capacity. Because repeated heat and chemical moulding wears the organs down, workers may purchase replacements. Official replacements are widely avaliable through markets owned and maintained by the elite. Each replacement lets a worker keep participating, keep producing.

Replacements are always available. Each successive organ may be a little lower in quality, and may fail a little faster than the last, but they keep the worker contributing to the labour economy. Owners may even offer **discounted organs in exchange for extra hours**, a convenient way to extend one's productivity and secure continued access to the system. All surgical and replacement costs are deducted directly from wages.

With The Kiln and the Organ Exchange Market, the body is no longer a limitation. It becomes adaptable, reshapeable, fully integrated into the machinery of modern industry.

**The future of labour is flexible. The future of productivity is the body itself.**
