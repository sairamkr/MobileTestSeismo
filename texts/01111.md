*By Pranjal & Paras*

in this age of automatoin, why scrabble for work when the earth itself can become your employment noticeboard?

**In the mountain extraction zones, seismic events are no longer disasters. They are opportunities!** Every tremor signals the start of of an unsettling that keeps the nation's vital energies flowing.

The **Seismic Rozgar Yojana (SRY)** assigns work the moment the ground moves. When seismic activity is detected, a list of tasks are registered: clear debris, stabilise a tunnel, reset an extraction valve. With each action helping restore the material and commercial systems that power cities far beyond the mountain -city ruined by the earthquake. Complete your task and your payments are transferred instantly to your bank account through the national digital-payment infrastructure. **No waiting. No paperwork. Just work when the earth calls.**

Earthquakes have always been woven into the structure of labour. Continuous extraction beneath the mountains destabilises the terrain, causing frequent tremors. Machines stop. Systems jam. Infrastructure fails. When technology collapses, human labour becomes the only reliable way to restore operations. 
SRY was built to coordinate this emergency (And increasingly, only) workforce. Its mechanical core lets tasks be distributed even when power and communications are down; each device links to a central labour-management system that logs completed work and releases wages automatically.

**Work is no longer scheduled by time or shift. It is triggered by disruption.** Every earthquake becomes a signal that labour is required.

Resource extraction has long turned remote landscapes into sites of production while leaving local communities economically dependent on the very industries that destabilise their environment. The community is already economically bound to disruption.

The speculation asks a simple question: **what happens when systems profit from instability — when the shaking of the earth becomes the only time meaningful work appears?**
