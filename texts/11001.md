*By Priyanka*

**Paying with money is a thing of the past — now you can pay with body parts,** *always available and extremely convenient in a cash crunch!*

If you can't afford rent, groceries, or electricity, lease parts of your body to companies that need physical labour. *Your arms might sort packages, your legs might walk warehouse aisles, your hands might assemble products you'll never see.*

The work happens inside monitored corporate facilities called Labor Centers. You check in, sign a legal agreement, and lie down in a small pod. A neural interface connects to the base of your neck; within minutes the brain drops into a passive state, close to sleep. While the conscious mind rests, the company's AI takes temporary control of specific muscle groups through the nervous system. The body stands, walks, lifts, and repeats for hours, guided by software that treats muscle like programmable hardware.

When the session ends, you wake in the pod with minor aftereffects like soreness, disorientation, organ failure, or just the subtle sense of time having skipped forward. Your account is credited, and the generous payment might cover food, housing, or transport for the week.

Companies call it efficient. Building millions of robots is expensive and slow; human bodies already exist everywhere, repair themselves, adapt, and run on simple energy. **Renting bodies is cheaper than building machines.**

*Students lease their bodies overnight to pay tuition. Workers rent out their legs for walking shifts while they rest in the pod.* Officially, body leasing is permitted only between individuals and certified corporate AI systems; renting your body directly to another person is illegal, though underground exchanges let people access these amenities of modernity without the state's interference.

All of it grows naturally from what was once called the gig economy, from the great companies of the heyday like Zomato, Uber. If we're already moving toward a world where we no longer own things, why should our bodies be any different? We subscribe to software, stream entertainment, lease vehicles, rent homes. **Access is replacing ownership. So let others access your body, if you're not using it.**

Perhaps it's finally time to stop outsourcing just the small decisions to algorithms and commit fully to the paradigm: **why not stop using technology, and start becoming the biological hardware for it?**
