*What's your source of information? Friends? Social media? Gossip? That lizard on your kitchen cabinet? Or perhaps that strange old artefact from the yesteryears, the News?*

Traditional news channels, newspapers, magazines, even the venerable radio — are now out of commission, outcompeted by more accessible media like social platforms. People no longer make time to follow the news, and the traditional outlets had no choice but to shut down.

The few who still cared, and still preferred the old medium, agreed to work in secrecy to deliver information, we call them whistleblowers of the news. **Undisclosed News works with the government to distribute information**, having accepted that consumers won't read a newspaper or watch a bulletin.

How? By outsourcing it to seemingly unsuspecting influencers. **Factual information is turned into a guessing game: spot the one truth among four lies.** Being lied to, consumers are forced to actually think about the news for the first time.

*1. "India needs to take a side. Hiding behind Russia is cowardly," says US President Donald Trump amid the ongoing war.*
*2. The US Ministry of External Affairs debunks rumours of using Indian ports to strike Iran; the US Navy claims it used its own sea channels.*
*3. Trump says "it's too late to stop the war" as Iranian Prime Minister Abdul Majeed Hakeem Ilahi demands a retreat.*

**True or false?**

We want readers to feel how much it matters to sift crucial information from noise — and what better way than to speak the one language that seems to proliferate online: lies. Our short attention span, and our reluctance to pay for shared, fundamental truths, will quietly erode our ability to agree on the state of the world we share. And if news traces the shape of reality, then the misinformation we consume may already be redrawing its contours.

**So here we are, asking: why not learn to lie with precision? To challenge the very doctrine propping up every chatroom and Reddit thread? Why not question how we retrieve information, by imagining a future where the news is hidden and truth must be hunted, as perhaps it already must?**
