*By Reet*

**What if your toilet knew more about you than your family, your friends, or your browser history?**

Every time someone flushes, the system learns a little more about how its citizens think. Here, information is treated like food: people gorge on articles, reels, memes, podcasts, and opinion threads, and their bodies digest it all. What remains is **cognitive waste**, a physical residue that reveals exactly what a person has been consuming. Every public toilet is fitted with sensors and wired into a national monitoring network that analyses the waste on flush and issues an **Intellectual Health Report**. A receipt might read: *"User consumed 23 reels, 5 memes, 1 opinion article. Cognitive fibre critically low. Suggestion: try reading something longer than a caption."*

Beneath the city, the sewers have been rebuilt as combined treatment plants and data centres. Streams of cognitive waste run through pipelines worked by the country's most valued labourers: the **Cognitive Scavengers**. Once the most despised of workers, are now celebrated as heroes! Hired by intelligence agencies to descend into the pipes and recover the fragments of thought that machines fail to detect, tracing the residue of politicians, scientists, journalists, and activists. When a particular pipeline draws the interest of national security, they work undercover, quite literally beneath the city.

Occasionally a sample is deemed historically significant and preserved in the **National Museum of Intellectual Waste**. Inside glass jars sit the cognitive remains of the famous - exhibits such as *"Residual thought fragments of a Nobel Prize-winning scientist, hours before announcing her breakthrough."* Schoolchildren on field trips wander the aisles of preserved thought, studying the strange afterlife of ideas that were once simply flushed away.
